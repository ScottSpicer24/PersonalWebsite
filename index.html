My Story

In the fall of 2017, I decided to pursue a degree in industrial engineering at the University of Central Florida, where I gained knowledge in manufacturing, statistics, and process improvement. During my studies, I had the opportunity to intern at the Frito-Lay manufacturing plant in Casa Grande, Arizona, where my focus was on reducing water usage. Upon graduating in December 2021, I began working at the Frito-Lay manufacturing plant in Orlando, where I was managing the maintenance team on the night shift.

After working there for a while, I quickly realized that the position was more focused on management rather than engineering. This realization prompted me to contemplate returning to the engineering field. During this period of contemplation, I recalled how much I enjoyed my computer science classes in college. As a result, I began teaching myself programming while continuing to work. I found immense satisfaction in this pursuit, which ultimately led me to make the decision to quit my job and pursue computer science full-time by returning to school.
